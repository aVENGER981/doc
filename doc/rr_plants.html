Component summary

This component brings plant creatures, animals and magical beasts closer to their PnP counterparts. Legitimately missing abilities are restored and spuriously granted benefits are removed. For this purpose, the AD&D Monstrous Manual and the Monstrous Compendium were used as reference. The creatures' AI has been improved as well, and it now allows them to act in accordance to the behavior described in the source books. In effect, these changes may slightly increase the difficulty of dealing with the affected creatures.

Plant creatures

Plant creatures are composed of vegetable material, but unlike normal plants these monsters may be carnivorous, intelligent, mobile or any combination of the three. Some plant creatures came into existence as a result of arcane influences while others may have evolved naturally.

New spell-like abilities and special traits

As per PnP rules, creatures cannot be interrupted while using their spell-like abilities, all of which have a casting time of 1. In all other aspects, spell-like abilities function exactly like the spells which they mimic unless otherwise noted in their description.

Acid Spit
Ankhegs can spit acid at a single creature within 30 feet dealing 8d4 points of acid damage. A successful saving throw vs. poison halves the damage.
Burrow
Creatures with this ability can conceal their presence by burrowing into the ground. In doing so, they become hidden from normal view until they make an attack. Detect Invisibility, True Seeing and similar spells can reveal a burrowed creature.
Concealment
Creatures with this ability can conceal their presence by seamlessly blending into their natural surroundings. In doing so, they become completely hidden from normal view until they make an attack. Detect Invisibility, True Seeing and similar spells can reveal a concealed creature.
Constrict
A creature with this ability can constrict an opponent dealing additional damage and holding it in place for 1 round unless a save vs. breath is made. While constricted, the victim cannot take any actions besides trying to escape. Large and incorporeal creatures cannot be constricted.
Electricity Absorption
A creature with this ability gains 8 extra hit points (up to a maximum of 32) whenever hit by electrical damage. Any excess electrical damage is absorbed and heals the creature.
Fire Breath
Hell Hounds can breathe fire at a single opponent within 30 feet causing 7 points of fire damage. A successful save vs. breath reduces the damage by half.
Frost Breath
Winter Wolves can release a cone-shaped stream of frost from their lungs causing 6d4 points of cold damage to all creatures within 10 feet. A successful save vs. breath reduces the damage by half.
Gaze of Pestilence
Any creature meeting the gaze of a Boalisk must save vs. petrification or become infected with a magical rotting disease, identical to that inflicted by a Mummy.
Hallucinator Spores
A Myconid can direct this spore cloud at one living creature. If that creature fails its saving throw vs. poison, it suffers violent hallucinations, becoming confused for 1 turn. Constructs, elementals, slimes and undead creatures are unaffected by the spore cloud.
Pacifier Spores
A Myconid can direct this spore cloud at one living creature. If the creature fails its saving throw vs. poison, it becomes totally passive, unable to do anything during the next 5 rounds. The victim is effectively feeble-minded, loses any dexterity bonus to its AC and cannot perform any actions, even if it is attacked. Constructs, elementals, slimes and undead creatures are unaffected by the spore cloud.
Petrifying Gaze
When a creature made of flesh meets the gaze of a Basilisk it must save vs. petrification or become turned to stone. Creatures not made of flesh such as skeletal undead, oozes, elementals and constructs made from are not affected by the gaze.
Plant Traits
All plant creatures are immune to: poison, disease, bleeding, polymorph, stun and sleep. Due to their unique physiology, plant creatures are not subject to backstab attacks, critical hits nor the Mind Flayers' Devour Brain attack. For the same reason, they are unaffected by Flesh to Stone, Insect Plague and similar spells. All plant creatures possess innate infravision.
Poison_Breath
A Greater Basilisk's breath is extremely poisonous. Anyone within 5 feet of the creature must save vs. poison (with a +2 bonus) or take 120 points of poison damage (4 per second).
Poisonous Skin
The skin of a Myconid King is covered by a highly poisonous substance. Anyone attempting to strike the creature in melee must save vs. poison or take 20 points of poison damage (1 per second).
Rake
A creature with this ability can rake an opponent with its rear claws dealing extra damage.
Spore Explosion
If a Gas Spore is struck for even 1 point of damage it explodes in a violent blast of gas that deals 6d6 points of missile damage to anyone within a 20-foot radius. Furthermore, all living creatures within that area become infected by the released spore cloud taking 3 points of disease damage per round until they receive a Cure Disease spell. The victims can save vs. wands to reduce the explosion damage by half and avoid becoming infected.
Sprint
A creature with this ability can run at three times its base speed for 3 rounds.
Stealth
Creatures with this ability can hide in shadows and move silently in order to avoid detection until they make an attack. It is not possible to use stealth while engaged in combat nor while opponents are actively watching.
Suffocate
Creatures with this ability can suffocate an entangled or helpless victim unless it makes a save vs. death. Creatures that do not breathe (such as undead, elementals and constructs) cannot be suffocated.
Tremorsense
Creature with this ability are sensitive to vibrations in the earth and can automatically pinpoint the location of anything that is in contact with the ground. A creature with tremorsense can detect invisible opponents and is unaffected by blindness.

Altered Spells

Animal Summoning I now summons up to five animals of 4 HD or less. Speficically, this spell will summon up to 5 specimens of either of the following animals: Black Bear, Jaguar, Panther, Dire Wolf

Animal Summoning II now summons up to five animals of 8 HD or less. Speficically, this spell will summon either 5 Polar Bears or 5 Saber-tooth Tigers

Animal Summoning III now summons up to 4 animals of 16 HD or less. Speficically, this spell will summon 4 Giant Bears.

Conjure Animals now lasts 2 rounds/level and summons 2 Cave Bears at level 12 and 2 Polar Bears at level 16 and 1 Giant Bear at level 20. As per PnP rules, this spell is no longer available to Druids. However, it should be noted that Animal Summoning II and III are now superior to it in every way.

A summoned animal can now be dismissed by selecting "Unsummon" from the creature's special abilities menu.

More sensible encounters

This part of the component slightly alters the placement of animals, magical beasts and plant creatures in certain areas in order to provide a more immersive gameplay experience. Here's the full list of changes:

  • Druid Grove (AR1900) - the Spore Colonies and the accompanying Myconids are replaced with a Shambling Mound and two Lesser Shamblers. This was done because fungus-based subterranean creatures do not willingly expose themselves to direct sunlight.

Compatibility

  • This component uses Detectable Spells and is therefore incompatible with the Improved Anvil mod
  • In order to achieve optimal compatibility with Sword Coast Stratagems I & II, Spell Revisions, Tactics and Big Picture, you should install aTweaks after those mods.
